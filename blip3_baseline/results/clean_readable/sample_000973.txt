Task: Evolution of Agricultural Working Dogs
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Domestication of dogs began thousands of years ago, evolving alongside human agricultural practices.
2) Early dogs were primarily used for hunting and guarding livestock.
3) As agriculture developed, dogs were trained to assist in tasks like herding and cart pulling.
4) Over time, specific breeds emerged that excelled in particular roles within farming communities.
5) These working dogs adapted to diverse climates and terrains, enhancing their utility in various agricultural settings.
6) Modern agricultural techniques have led to changes in dog breeds' roles, focusing more on specialized tasks such as tracking and detecting.
7) Today, some breeds continue to serve in traditional roles, while others have been repurposed for new agricultural needs.
