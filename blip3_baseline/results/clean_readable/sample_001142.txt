Task: Growing Hot Peppers
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Start by selecting a suitable growing location that receives at least six hours of direct sunlight daily.
2)  Choose hot pepper varieties based on your climate and personal preference for heat level.
3)  Prepare the soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure to improve fertility and drainage.
4)  Plant seeds indoors four to six weeks before the last expected frost date.
5)  Transplant seedlings outdoors when nighttime temperatures remain above 50°F (10°C).
6)  Space plants about 18 to 24 inches apart to allow for growth and air circulation.
7)  Water plants deeply once or twice weekly, ensuring the soil stays consistently moist but not waterlogged.
8)  Apply a balanced fertilizer every four to six weeks to support healthy growth.
9)  Monitor plants regularly for pests and diseases, addressing issues promptly.
10)  Harvest peppers when they reach their desired size and color, which can vary by variety.
