Task: The Basics of Organic Growing Strawberries (in the Tropics)
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil for planting strawberries.
2)  Prepare the soil by adding compost or organic matter to improve fertility.
3)  Plant strawberry plants in early spring after the last frost.
4)  Space plants about 18 inches apart to allow for growth.
5)  Water plants regularly, ensuring they receive at least 1 inch of water per week.
6)  Mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7)  Apply a balanced fertilizer every two months during growing season.
8)  Monitor for pests and diseases, using natural remedies if necessary.
9)  Harvest strawberries when they are fully ripe and easy to pull from the plant.
10)  Encourage fruit production by removing flowers on young plants until they produce berries.
11)  Consider using floating row covers to protect plants from birds and insects.
12)  Rotate strawberry beds annually to prevent soil-borne diseases.
