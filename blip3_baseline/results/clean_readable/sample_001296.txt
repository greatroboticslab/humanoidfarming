Task: Tips for Growing Peppers | From the Ground Up
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Choose pepper varieties suitable for your climate.
2)  Start seeds indoors six to eight weeks before last frost.
3)  Plant seedlings outdoors after all danger of frost has passed.
4)  Space plants about 18 inches apart in well-draining soil.
5)  Water regularly, keeping soil consistently moist but not soggy.
6)  Feed plants every two weeks with a balanced fertilizer.
7)  Pinch off the tips of young plants to encourage bushier growth.
8)  Support plants with stakes or cages as they grow taller.
9)  Harvest peppers when they reach full color and firmness.
10)  Keep plants healthy by removing yellow leaves and weeds.
11)  Consider using row covers for protection against pests and cold.
12)  Monitor for diseases and treat promptly if necessary.
