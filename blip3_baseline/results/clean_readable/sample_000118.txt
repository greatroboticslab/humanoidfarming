1) The monsoon season brings crucial rainfall to India, sustaining agriculture and ecosystems.
2) It occurs annually between June and September due to warm air currents from the Indian Ocean.
3) Precipitation varies significantly across regions, affecting water availability and crop yields.
4) Monsoons influence temperature patterns, contributing to seasonal climate changes.
5) They play a vital role in India's economy by supporting over 50% of its food production.
6) However, extreme weather events during monsoons can lead to flooding and landslides.
7) Climate change is altering monsoon patterns, posing challenges for sustainable development.
8) Understanding monsoon dynamics helps in better planning and disaster preparedness measures.
