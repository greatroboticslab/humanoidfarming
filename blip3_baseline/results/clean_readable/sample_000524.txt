------------------------------------------------------------
Foot-and-mouth disease is highly contagious among cloven-hoofed animals.
It affects cattle, pigs, sheep, and other livestock species.
The virus spreads through direct contact between infected and susceptible animals.
Transmission can also occur via contaminated feed, water, or equipment.
Symptoms include blisters on the mouth, feet, and udder of affected animals.
Humans can contract the disease but are not commonly affected.
Vaccination programs are used in some regions to control the spread of the disease.
Control measures include quarantining infected areas and monitoring for symptoms.
Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and preventing secondary infections.
