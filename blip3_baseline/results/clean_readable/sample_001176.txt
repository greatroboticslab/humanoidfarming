Task: Easy DIY Worm Farm | Worm Farming for Beginners | Worm Farm Anywhere | Hey It's A Good Life
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Choose a suitable container for your worm farm, such as a plastic bin or aquarium.
2)  Line the bottom of the container with shredded newspaper or cardboard for bedding.
3)  Add a layer of moist organic material like leaves or grass to provide food scraps.
4)  Place red wiggler worms in the bin, about 100-200 per square foot.
5)  Start feeding the worms fruit and vegetable scraps from your kitchen.
6)  Cover the bin with a breathable lid to keep out pests and maintain humidity.
7)  Check the moisture level weekly and adjust if necessary by adding more bedding or scraps.
8)  Harvest mature worms every few months by separating them from the bedding.
9)  Compost the remaining bedding and food scraps to use as fertilizer.
10)  Enjoy the rich soil you've created from your homemade compost.
