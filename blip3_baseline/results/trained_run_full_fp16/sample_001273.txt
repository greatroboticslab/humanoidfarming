Task: #723  tractor wheel bearing repair [Davidsfarm]
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Provide numbered subtasks with concise justifications.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify the type of tractor wheel bearing issue from symptoms like noise or vibration.
2) Gather necessary tools including wrenches, socket set, and grease gun.
3) Drain any remaining oil from the wheel hub before removing the bearing.
4) Use a pry bar to pop out the old bearing if stuck, then remove the wheel.
5) Clean the hub and shaft thoroughly with solvent to prepare for new bearing.
6) Select an appropriate replacement bearing that matches size and condition.
7) Install the new bearing onto the shaft, ensuring proper alignment and tight fit.
8) Reassemble the wheel and hub, applying fresh lubricant as needed.
9) Test drive the tractor to check if the problem has been resolved.
10) If issues persist, consult a professional mechanic for further diagnosis.
