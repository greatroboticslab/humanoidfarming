Task: Autumn Awakening (and Garden Graveyard!): Preparing for Cool-Season Planting
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Include validation steps and fallback procedures.
------------------------------------------------------------
Prepare garden beds for planting.
Choose cool-season plants suitable for your climate.
Collect seeds or seedlings of chosen plants.
Plant seeds or seedlings at recommended depths and spacings.
Water newly planted seeds or seedlings thoroughly.
Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
Monitor plants regularly for signs of pests or diseases.
Fertilize plants as needed based on their specific nutrient requirements.
Prune dead or diseased parts of plants to promote healthy growth.
Prepare containers if planting in pots or hanging baskets.
Ensure all tools and equipment are clean and ready for use.
Check weather forecasts for potential frost warnings.
Adjust irrigation schedule according to changing weather conditions.
Rotate crops to prevent depletion of soil nutrients.
Maintain consistent soil moisture levels throughout the growing season.
Inspect plants weekly for any signs of stress or damage.
Remove spent flowers to encourage more blooms.
Harvest vegetables and flowers as they mature.
Clean up fallen leaves and debris from the garden bed.
Store unused seeds in a cool, dry place until next planting season.
Dispose of diseased plant material properly to prevent spread of pathogens.
Document successful plantings and note areas needing improvement.
Plan future garden layouts based on this year's experiences.
Celebrate the success of your cool-season planting efforts.
