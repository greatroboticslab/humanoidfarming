Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Focus on safety, measurement units, and necessary tools.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify the optimal planting time for sunflowers based on local climate conditions.
2) Choose sunflower varieties suitable for U.S. and Canadian growing regions.
3) Prepare the soil by adding compost and adjusting pH levels as needed.
4) Space seeds at least 18 inches apart in rows that are 36 inches apart.
5) Water plants deeply once or twice weekly, depending on weather conditions.
6) Monitor for pests and diseases regularly, applying appropriate treatments if necessary.
7) Apply fertilizer according to package instructions during the growing season.
8) Harvest when heads reach full size and show signs of ripening, typically after 90 days from planting.
9) Cut the entire head off the plant using pruning shears or scissors.
10) Store harvested sunflower seeds in a cool, dry place until ready to use or roast.
11) Enjoy your freshly harvested sunflower seeds as a snack or use them in recipes.
12) Consider saving some seeds for next year's planting to ensure variety and quality.
