Task: Growing and harvesting large quantities of onions - Advanced agricultural techniques
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify suitable onion varieties for local climate and soil conditions.
2) Prepare land by plowing and removing weeds to ensure clean planting area.
3) Apply organic fertilizers or compost to enrich soil nutrients.
4) Plant seeds or seedlings at appropriate depth and spacing according to variety guidelines.
5) Water regularly until plants reach height of about six inches.
6) Mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7) Monitor for pests and diseases, using natural remedies if possible.
8) Thin out overcrowded plants to allow proper air circulation and sunlight exposure.
9) Support taller plants with stakes to prevent lodging during heavy rain.
10) Begin harvesting when outer leaves start to yellow and bulbs reach desired size.
11) Dig carefully to avoid damaging remaining bulbs and roots.
12) Cure harvested onions in a warm, dry place for several weeks before storage.
13) Store cured onions in cool, dark locations away from direct sunlight.
14) Rotate crops annually to maintain soil health and reduce pest buildup.
15) Consider implementing crop rotation every three years to enhance soil fertility.
