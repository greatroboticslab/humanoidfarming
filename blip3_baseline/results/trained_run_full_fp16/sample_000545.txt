Task: Agricultural Practices- Soil preparation and Transplantation.

1) Assess soil quality for necessary amendments like compost or fertilizer.
2) Till the soil to a depth of at least 12 inches for optimal root growth.
3) Level the prepared area to ensure even planting conditions.
4) Water the soil thoroughly before planting to settle the ground.
5) Choose appropriate seeds or seedlings based on climate and season.
6) Space plants according to their recommended distance for proper growth.
7) Gently handle young plants to avoid damaging roots during transplantation.
8) Dig holes slightly larger than the plant's root ball to prevent root damage.
9) Place the plant in the hole and fill it with soil, gently pressing down around the base.
10) Water newly transplanted plants immediately after planting to settle the soil.
11) Apply mulch around the base of the plant to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
12) Monitor plants closely for signs of stress or disease post-transplantation.
