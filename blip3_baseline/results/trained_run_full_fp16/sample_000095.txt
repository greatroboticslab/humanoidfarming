Task: Tomato Seedling Potting: How to Repot for Thriving Growth!
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Choose a pot larger than the current one to accommodate new growth.
2) Fill the bottom of the new pot with drainage material like gravel.
3) Add potting soil to the new pot until it's about two-thirds full.
4) Gently remove the tomato seedling from its old pot without damaging roots.
5) Place the seedling in the center of the new pot, ensuring proper root placement.
6) Fill around the seedling with additional potting soil, pressing gently.
7) Water the newly potted tomato seedling thoroughly to settle the soil.
8) Place the pot in a sunny spot receiving at least six hours of direct sunlight daily.
9) Monitor soil moisture levels, watering when needed to keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
10) Apply balanced fertilizer every four weeks during growing season for optimal nutrition.
11) Prune stems regularly to encourage bushier growth and prevent overcrowding.
12) Watch for signs of pests or diseases and address them promptly to maintain healthy plants.
