Sowing garlic on 28th September
2024) 
1) Choose a sunny spot in your garden for planting garlic.
2) Prepare the soil by removing weeds and adding compost if necessary.
3) Break open garlic bulbs carefully to separate individual cloves.
4) Plant garlic cloves about 4 inches deep and 6 inches apart in rows.
5) Water the planted garlic thoroughly to settle the soil around the cloves.
6) Cover the area where you planted garlic with mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7) Monitor the garlic plants for signs of pests or diseases and take appropriate action if needed.
8) Harvest garlic when the leaves start to yellow and die back naturally, usually late summer or early fall.
9) Store harvested garlic in a cool, dry place until ready to use.
10) Plan to plant new garlic cloves in spring for next year's harvest.
