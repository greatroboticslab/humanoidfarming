------------------------------------------------------------
1) Check if okras have reached their full size and color before harvesting.
2) Use pruning shears to cut the pods just above the soil level.
3) Pick okras regularly to encourage more production throughout the growing season.
4) Dispose of any damaged or diseased okra plants immediately.
5) Water the garden thoroughly after harvesting to keep the soil moist.
6) Rotate crops annually to prevent soil-borne diseases and pests.
7) Apply mulch around okra plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
8) Monitor for signs of pests or diseases and take appropriate action.
9) Consider using organic methods like neem oil or insecticidal soap if needed.
10) Enjoy fresh okra from your garden in various dishes like stews or stir-fries.
