Task: Cultivate Your Own Edamame: A Guide to Growing and Enjoying Soybeans!
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Include validation steps and fallback procedures.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Choose a sunny spot for your edamame plants.
2) Prepare the soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure.
3) Plant seeds about an inch deep and two feet apart in rows.
4) Water regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged.
5) Mulch around the plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
6) Support taller plants with stakes or trellises as they grow.
7) Harvest when pods turn green and seeds inside are plump.
8) Enjoy fresh edamame directly from the plant or dry for later use.
9) Save some seeds from your harvest to plant next year.
10) Rotate crops annually to prevent soil depletion and pest buildup.
