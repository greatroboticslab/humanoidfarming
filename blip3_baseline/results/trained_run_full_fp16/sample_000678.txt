------------------------------------------------------------
Harvesting Mini Bok Choy
- Cabbage
1) Water the plants thoroughly the day before harvesting to ensure they are well-hydrated.
2) Choose a sunny morning when temperatures are cool to avoid wilting the leaves.
3) Use sharp scissors or pruning shears to cut the heads just above the soil line.
4) Gently remove any remaining roots and debris from around the base of each plant.
5) Sort the harvested bok choy based on size and quality for best use in cooking.
6) Store the bok choy in a plastic bag in the refrigerator to keep fresh for up to a week.
7) If not using immediately, blanch the bok choy briefly in boiling water before freezing for later use.
8) Clean the cutting board and tools thoroughly after harvesting to prevent contamination.
9) Dispose of any discarded plant material responsibly away from water sources.
10) Enjoy your freshly harvested mini bok choy in various recipes like stir-fries or soups.
