Task: Growing Hot Peppers
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Provide numbered subtasks with concise justifications.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Start by choosing a suitable growing location that receives at least six hours of direct sunlight daily.
2) Select hot pepper varieties appropriate for your climate and soil type.
3) Prepare the soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure to improve fertility and drainage.
4) Plant seeds indoors four to six weeks before the last expected frost date.
5) Transplant seedlings outdoors after all danger of frost has passed.
6) Space plants about 18 to 24 inches apart for proper growth.
7) Water regularly, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
8) Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
9) Support plants with stakes or cages as they grow taller.
10) Monitor for pests and diseases, treating them promptly if necessary.
11) Harvest peppers when they reach their full color and have a firm texture.
12) Continue to care for plants until they finish producing in the season.
