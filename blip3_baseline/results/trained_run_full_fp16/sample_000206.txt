Task: Improving Your Soil One Amendment at a Time | From the Ground Up
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify the type of soil in your garden or plot.
2) Research which amendments are suitable for your specific soil type.
3) Determine the amount of each amendment needed based on soil test results.
4) Choose the best time to apply amendments for optimal absorption.
5) Prepare the soil by tilling or mixing amendments thoroughly.
6) Apply the recommended amount of amendments evenly across the area.
7) Water the amended soil deeply to help settle the amendments and encourage root growth.
8) Monitor the soil's health over time and adjust as necessary based on ongoing tests.
9) Consider companion planting alongside amended areas to enhance overall soil fertility.
10) Regularly maintain the soil by adding organic matter and composting kitchen scraps.
