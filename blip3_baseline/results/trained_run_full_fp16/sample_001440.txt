Task: Growing corn in garden organically | How-2-Do It Guide Step By Step | Best Guide
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Choose a sunny spot in your garden for planting corn.
2) Prepare the soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure to enrich it.
3) Plant corn seeds about two inches deep and six inches apart in rows spaced eight feet apart.
4) Water the plants deeply once a week, ensuring they receive at least an inch of water per week.
5) Mulch around the base of the corn plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
6) Support taller corn stalks with stakes or trellises as they grow.
7) Monitor for pests and diseases regularly, using organic methods if necessary.
8) Harvest corn when the kernels are plump and milky but not mushy.
9) Rotate crops each year to prevent soil depletion and reduce pest buildup.
10) Consider companion planting with marigolds or basil to deter pests naturally.
11) Apply organic fertilizers like fish emulsion or seaweed extract every few weeks during growing season.
12) Allow corn stalks to dry after harvest before removing them from the garden bed.
13) Store harvested corn in a cool, dry place until ready to use.
14) Plan next year's crop based on this year's performance and local climate conditions.
15) Share your organic gardening knowledge with others through community workshops or online forums.
