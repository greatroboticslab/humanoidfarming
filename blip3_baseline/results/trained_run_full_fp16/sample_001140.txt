Task: Pumpkin Growing Basics | From the Ground Up
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Focus on safety, measurement units, and necessary tools.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Choose a suitable location for your pumpkin patch that receives at least six hours of sunlight daily.
2) Prepare the soil by removing weeds and adding compost to enrich its nutrients.
3) Plant seeds about one inch deep in rows spaced ten feet apart.
4) Water plants deeply once or twice weekly, depending on weather conditions.
5) Monitor for pests and diseases regularly, using natural remedies when possible.
6) Support vines with stakes or trellises as they grow taller.
7) Harvest pumpkins when the skin is hard and the color has fully developed.
8) Store harvested pumpkins in a cool, dry place until ready to use.
9) Rotate crops annually to prevent soil-borne diseases and maintain fertility.
10) Consider planting companion plants like marigolds to deter pests naturally.
11) Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
12) Prune excess leaves to improve air circulation and reduce disease risk.
13) Encourage pollination by hand if necessary, especially during early bloom periods.
14) Plan for succession planting to ensure continuous harvest throughout the season.
15) Evaluate performance each year to make adjustments for future planting seasons.
