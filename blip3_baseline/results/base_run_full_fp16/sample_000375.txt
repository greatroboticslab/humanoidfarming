Task: Starting a Food Forest on the Cheap!
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Research local food forest designs and sustainable gardening practices.
2)  Identify suitable land with good soil quality and sunlight exposure.
3)  Assess available resources for tools, seeds, and compost.
4)  Plan the layout of trees, shrubs, and ground covers based on seasonal growth patterns.
5)  Obtain permission from landowners or authorities to use the space.
6)  Start small with easy-to-grow native plants and herbs.
7)  Monitor plant health regularly for signs of pests or diseases.
8)  Compost organic waste to enrich the soil without using chemical fertilizers.
9)  Water plants according to their needs, avoiding overwatering.
10)  Encourage beneficial insects and birds to help control pests naturally.
11)  Harvest fruits, vegetables, and herbs responsibly to maintain biodiversity.
12)  Evaluate the success of your food forest and make adjustments as needed.
