1) Choose a suitable container for your worm farm, ensuring it has a lid to keep pests out.
2) Line the bottom of the container with shredded newspaper or cardboard for bedding.
3) Add a layer of moist leaves or compost on top of the bedding for food waste.
4) Introduce red wiggler worms into the container, following package instructions carefully.
5) Gradually introduce kitchen scraps like fruit peels and vegetable trimmings as food.
6) Maintain proper moisture levels and temperature in the worm farm environment.
7) Harvest mature worms regularly, separating them from the castings for use in gardening.
8) Use the worm castings as natural fertilizer for plants, enriching soil health.
