Task: Irrigation Soil & Your Garden
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Focus on safety, measurement units, and necessary tools.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Assess your garden's soil type to determine its water retention capacity.
2) Measure your garden's area in square meters for accurate irrigation needs.
3) Gather necessary tools including a hose, timer, and a trowel for testing soil.
4) Check local climate conditions to adjust watering frequency accordingly.
5) Install a drip irrigation system or soaker hoses for efficient water delivery.
6) Set up a rain gauge to monitor natural rainfall and adjust irrigation timing.
7) Use a moisture meter to check soil moisture levels before watering deeply.
8) Water early in the morning to reduce evaporation and promote plant growth.
9) Adjust sprinkler heads to ensure even coverage across your garden beds.
10) Monitor plants for signs of overwatering or underwatering to make adjustments.
