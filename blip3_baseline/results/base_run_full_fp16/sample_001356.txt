Task: Chives The Profitable Short Season Crop #gardening  #financial #Farming #Agriculture #agribusiness
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Focus on safety, measurement units, and necessary tools.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify suitable chive varieties for short season planting.
2) Choose a well-drained soil rich in organic matter.
3) Prepare the soil by tilling to a depth of at least 15 cm.
4) Plant chives in rows spaced 30 cm apart.
5) Water plants deeply once a week during dry periods.
6) Apply a balanced fertilizer every two weeks starting from spring.
7) Monitor for pests and diseases regularly.
8) Harvest chives when they reach about 10 cm tall.
9) Use scissors or pruning shears to cut stems close to the ground.
10) Store harvested chives in a cool, dry place for up to two weeks.
11) Plan succession planting for continuous harvest throughout the season.
12) Calculate expected yield based on plant spacing and variety.
13) Estimate costs for seeds, fertilizers, and labor per square meter.
14) Determine selling price per pound or per bunch for financial planning.
15) Set up a sales network through local markets or online platforms.
16) Track expenses and income monthly for financial health.
17) Adjust planting dates based on local climate forecasts.
18) Consider crop rotation to maintain soil fertility and reduce pest buildup.
19) Implement water conservation techniques such as drip irrigation.
20) Ensure all tools used are clean and sanitized before use.
