Task: THE SECRET TO PLANTING GARLIC FOR PROFIT (AND SAVING YOUR SEED)
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Choose garlic varieties suitable for your climate and soil type.
2)  Purchase disease-free garlic cloves from a reputable source.
3)  Prepare planting beds by tilling and adding compost for nutrient-rich soil.
4)  Plant garlic cloves in rows at a depth of about two inches, spacing them six to eight inches apart.
5)  Water the planted garlic thoroughly to settle the soil around the cloves.
6)  Mulch the bed with organic material like straw or leaves to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7)  Monitor for pests and diseases regularly, using natural methods if possible.
8)  Harvest garlic when the tops begin to yellow and fall over naturally, usually in late summer or early autumn.
9)  Clean harvested garlic by gently brushing off excess soil.
10)  Store garlic in a cool, dry place until ready to use or sell.
11)  Evaluate the yield and adjust planting techniques based on results for future success.
12)  Consider sharing surplus garlic with neighbors or selling at local markets to diversify income.
