Task: Garlic and Onion Harvest -  Cold Climate Gardening
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Provide numbered subtasks with concise justifications.
------------------------------------------------------------
1) Assess local cold climate gardening guidelines for garlic and onion planting dates.
2) Choose cold-resistant garlic and onion varieties suitable for the region.
3) Prepare garden beds by tilling soil to a depth of at least 12 inches.
4) Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure to improve soil fertility.
5) Plant garlic cloves about 4 inches apart in rows 18 inches apart.
6) Plant onion sets about 4 inches apart in rows 12 inches apart.
7) Water plants deeply after planting to settle the soil around the roots.
8) Mulch planted areas with straw or leaves to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
9) Monitor plants for signs of pests or diseases; apply organic treatments if necessary.
10) Thin garlic plants to 6 inches apart once they have sprouted.
11) Thin onion plants to 6 inches apart when bulbs begin to form.
12) Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4 weeks during the growing season.
13) Water plants regularly, especially during dry spells, ensuring consistent moisture.
14) Harvest garlic bulbs when the tops start to yellow and fall over naturally.
15) Harvest onions when the tops have completely dried and fallen over.
16) Store harvested garlic in a cool, dry place with good air circulation.
17) Store harvested onions in a cool, dark place with good air circulation.
18) Inspect stored garlic and onions periodically for signs of rot or infestation.
