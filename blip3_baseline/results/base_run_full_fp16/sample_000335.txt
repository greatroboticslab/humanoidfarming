Task: GROOVE YOUR TIRES & IMPROVE TRACTION: DIY FIX REALLY WORKS
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Focus on safety, measurement units, and necessary tools.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Inspect your tires for any cuts or punctures before starting.
2)  Ensure you have the correct tire pressure as per your vehicle's manual.
3)  Use a tire gauge to check and adjust the pressure if necessary.
4)  Measure the tread depth using a tread depth gauge or a credit card.
5)  Replace any tires that show less than 2/32 inch of tread depth.
6)  Check the wheel alignment to ensure proper tire contact with the road.
7)  Clean the tires and wheels thoroughly with a mild detergent.
8)  Apply tire dressing to protect the rubber from UV rays and wear.
9)  Inflate all tires to the recommended PSI level.
10)  Rotate your tires every 5,000 to 7,500 miles for even wear.
11)  Consider adding a tire chain or snow tire for improved traction in winter.
12)  Test drive your vehicle to ensure everything is working correctly.
