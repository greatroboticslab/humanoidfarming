Task: Raised Bed Garden. Learn as you grow
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Include validation steps and fallback procedures.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Research different types of raised bed gardens suitable for your climate and soil type.
2)  Choose a location that receives at least six hours of sunlight daily.
3)  Measure the space needed for your garden beds.
4)  Decide on the size and shape of your raised beds.
5)  Purchase materials such as lumber, soil, compost, and mulch.
6)  Build the raised beds using wood or other durable materials.
7)  Level the ground where the raised beds will be placed.
8)  Fill the beds with a mixture of topsoil and compost.
9)  Add a layer of mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
10)  Plant seeds or seedlings according to your chosen gardening plan.
11)  Water the plants regularly, ensuring they receive about an inch of water per week.
12)  Monitor plant growth and adjust watering as necessary.
13)  Apply organic fertilizers every few months to maintain soil health.
14)  Regularly remove weeds to prevent competition for nutrients and water.
15)  Harvest vegetables and flowers when they are ready.
16)  Rotate crops each season to avoid depleting soil nutrients.
17)  Inspect plants weekly for signs of pests or diseases.
18)  Adjust care practices based on feedback from plants' health and performance.
19)  Consider adding trellises or supports for climbing plants.
20)  Evaluate the success of your raised bed garden annually and make improvements.
