4) Monitor water quality and quantity before and after implementing new practices.
5) Educate community members about the importance of proper waste disposal.
6) Establish buffer zones around water bodies to prevent runoff contamination.
7) Regularly review and adjust practices based on effectiveness and feedback.

9) Implement early warning systems for potential flooding or droughts.
10) Encourage participation from all stakeholders including government agencies, NGOs, and private sectors.
11) Conduct periodic environmental impact assessments to ensure long-term sustainability.
12) Provide training sessions for farmers on efficient irrigation methods and pest control.
