Task: KNOW YOUR RASPBERRIES - How to get a BUMPER crop from your homegrown raspberries
Please break it into step-by-step subtasks. Emphasize precision, sustainability, and error checks.
------------------------------------------------------------
1)  Choose disease-resistant raspberry varieties suitable for your climate.
2)  Plant raspberry plants in well-draining soil enriched with compost.
3)  Water plants deeply but infrequently to encourage strong root growth.
4)  Mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
5)  Prune plants annually in late winter or early spring to promote air circulation.
6)  Apply organic fertilizers like fish emulsion or seaweed extract monthly.
7)  Harvest ripe berries regularly to encourage continuous fruiting.
8)  Use row covers to protect plants from pests during vulnerable stages.
9)  Rotate crops annually to prevent soil-borne diseases.
10)  Monitor for signs of pests or diseases and treat promptly with natural remedies.
11)  Encourage pollinators such as bees by planting nearby flowers.
12)  Harvest ripe berries by gently twisting or cutting them off the plant.
13)  Store harvested berries in the refrigerator to extend freshness.
