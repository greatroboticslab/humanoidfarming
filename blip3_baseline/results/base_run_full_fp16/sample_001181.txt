------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify suitable garlic varieties for fermentation.
2) Gather necessary materials like jars, cheesecloth, and lids.
3) Clean and prepare garlic heads thoroughly.
4) Place garlic in clean jars, leaving space at top.
5) Add salt or brine to cover garlic completely.
6) Seal jars tightly and store in a cool, dark place.
7) Monitor progress every few days for signs of fermentation.
8) Once fermented, strain out garlic cloves for use.
9) Use fermented garlic in various dishes or recipes.
10) Share excess with neighbors or local community.
