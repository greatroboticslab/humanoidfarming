------------------------------------------------------------
1) Identify suitable crops for no-till farming based on local climate and soil conditions.
2) Prepare a seed bed using minimal tillage to preserve soil structure.
3) Plant seeds directly into prepared soil without disturbing existing vegetation.
4) Apply crop rotation to maintain soil fertility without intensive plowing.
5) Monitor crops closely for early detection of pests and diseases.
6) Use cover crops to protect soil from erosion and improve nutrient levels.
7) Implement integrated pest management strategies to reduce chemical usage.
8) Harvest crops when mature to ensure optimal yield and quality.
9) Maintain soil moisture through appropriate irrigation techniques.
10) Evaluate farm performance annually to adjust practices as needed.
