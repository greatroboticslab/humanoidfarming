1) Start by assessing the garden's soil quality for nutrient content and pH balance.
2) Choose plants suitable for your climate zone to ensure they thrive.
3) Water plants deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth.
4) Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
5) Regularly prune dead or diseased plant parts to promote healthy growth.
6) Rotate crops annually to prevent soil depletion and reduce pest issues.
7) Use appropriate tools like pruners, trowels, and watering cans for precision.
8) Wear protective clothing such as gloves and long sleeves when gardening.
9) Keep tools clean and sharp to avoid injury and maintain efficiency.
10) Monitor plants for signs of pests or diseases and address promptly.
11) Consider companion planting to enhance plant health and deter pests naturally.
12) Plan for seasonal changes in plant care to adapt to weather fluctuations.
