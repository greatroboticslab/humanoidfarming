 What type of government is an animal farm? What type of government is an animal farm? This question takes us into a fascinating exploration of how power and ideals can change over time. Animal farm begins with a vision rooted in socialism. In this context, socialism means that the animals collectively own and manage the farm. They share the fruits of their labor and make decisions together. The initial system is called animalism, which serves as an analogy for socialism. Animalism is based on the idea that all animals are equal and should work together for the common good, free from the exploitation they suffered under human rule. At the start, the animals establish a firm of direct democracy. They gather to discuss and vote on important issues. The pigs, being the most intelligent, help organize the farm's operations. This early stage reflects the ideals of socialism, where the community owns the means of production, which in this case is the farm itself. The animals work toward shared goals, believing they can create a better society. However, as the story progresses, the government of Animal Farm changes significantly. The pigs, led by Napoleon, gradually take more power for themselves. They rewrite the rules, manipulate the other animals, and use propaganda to justify their actions. Over time, Animal Farm shifts from a socialist democracy to a totalitarian regime. In this new system, Napoleon and the pigs act as a ruling class, making all decisions and enforcing their will through fear and deception. The original ideals of equality and shared ownership are corrupted and abandoned. From a socialist perspective, Animal Farm serves as a warning about how revolutions can be undermined by those who seek power for themselves. The story shows that even when a society is founded on principles of equality and collective ownership, there is a risk that a new elite will emerge. This new elite can recreate the same oppressive structures they originally fought against. The transformation of Animal Farm's government highlights the challenges of maintaining socialist ideals in practice, especially when leaders become corrupt or authoritarian. In essence, Animal Farm starts with a government inspired by socialism, based on equality and collective ownership. However, it ends as a totalitarian dictatorship under the pigs' control. The story critiques how socialist revolutions can be betrayed from within, rather than rejecting socialism itself.
