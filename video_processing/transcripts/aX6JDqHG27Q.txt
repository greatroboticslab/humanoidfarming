 Nepal is an agricultural country where more than 60% of the population is engaged in farming. Here, local food production barely meets consumption, even though the country is able to produce enough vegetables to meet the national demand. To improve the situation and difficulties farmers face concerning access to irrigation solutions, water, energy, and financial services, the United Nations Capital Development Fund partnered with Sun Farmer, a solar-powered irrigation solutions company. Through this partnership, Sun Farmer established a one-acre model farm in Taha municipality, 30 kilometers outside of Kathmandu. The farm empowers smallholder farmers on the use of modern farming techniques. Here, they train the farmers in high-value and off-season crop cultivation, as well as tunnel farming. Farmers receive fertilizers and seeds and sell their produce to different markets through Sun Farmer. Sun Farmer Sun Farmer also introduced a solar water pump as an affordable pay-as-you-go irrigation solution. This solution allows farmers to have access to water all year long without the burden of a huge upfront cost. This is important considering much of Nepal's existing water supply infrastructure is still damaged from the 2015 earthquake. In Majigau, close to Thaha village, similar pay-as-you-go solar water pump for drinking water is benefitting 150 families. They are now able to save up to three hours a day simply because they do not have to hike multiple times to collect water. Suddenly, they tune in.
