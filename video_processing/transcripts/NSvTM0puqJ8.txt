 Agricultural Waste Management Agricultural waste is unwanted or unsalable materials produced wholly from agriculture operations directly related to the growing of crops or raising animals for the primary purpose of making profit or for livelihood. Agricultural waste is composed of organic wases such as animal excreta in the form of slurries and farmed manures spent in mushrooms composed of soiled water and silage effluent. Agricultural waste includes natural waste, animal waste, and plant waste. Field wases, weeds, and straws, animal wases, animal dung, or dead bodies. Agro-industrial wases such as sugar cane molasses and peels. Waste management is an important element of environment protection. Its purpose is to provide hygiene-efficient and economic solid waste storage collection, transport, and treatment of disposal of waste without polluting the atmosphere, soil, or water system. Composting has become a preferable option to treat organic wases. Composting is a method in which organic matter present in agricultural waste is decomposed by aerobically or anaerobically through a biochemical process and converted into humus.
