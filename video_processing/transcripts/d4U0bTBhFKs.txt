 The agricultural industry is changing rapidly. People working in the agricultural science and technology solve some of the most complex problems in the world today. One of the most important problems our world faces is efficiently producing, processing, and distributing food for a growing human population. BYU-Idaho students in agriculture science and technology find careers working on the applied side of technology and engineering in agriculture. They take what has been developed and find new ways to apply technology in agriculture that will have an impact on a global scale. The BYU-Idaho Agriculture Technology program helps prepare each of its students by implementing opportunities for problem solving, using hands-on applications, and incorporating real-world experiences in the classroom, lab, and field. Agricultural college graduates are in high demand and have a variety of career paths to choose from. As you consider opportunities for career paths, be sure to look at what you can do with a degree in Agricultural Science and Technology. Career opportunities include machinery manager, restoring or handling crops, and managing retail or processing facilities. BYU-Idaho is proud to be a part of preparing the next generation of leaders in the Agricultural Science and Technology industry.
