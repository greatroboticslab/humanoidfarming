 Do you know how to increase the yield of bell peppers? Bell peppers tend to have many lateral branches that grow weakly, thus failing to meet the nutritional needs of the fruits. To achieve high yields and large fruits, it's crucial to remove small and misshapen fruits from the lateral branches, leaving only the fruits on the main branches. Typically, keep three to four main branches and allow three fruits to grow on each branch. Once these three fruits ripen, leave another three fruits to grow on top. After the main branches of bell peppers bear fruits, they tend to grow lateral branches at the fruiting positions. When these lateral branches emerge, pinch the tips after leaving three leaves. Bell peppers are shallow-rooted plants with no taproot, only fibrous roots. They enjoy water but are sensitive to waterlogging and are fond of fertilizers but susceptible to over-fertilization.
