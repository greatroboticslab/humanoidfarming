 Known as a food source and social culture wealth symbol, Indignas cattle have been known to support a wide range of Ugandan families. Although their phenotypes are well suited to their agroecological zones with traditional management practices, they do not provide the competitive produce to challenge the market. With intentions to increase productivity, the Indignas cattle keepers are upgrading the genotypes towards high-yielding exotic dairy cattle. This week, the pros of commercializing the Indignas cattle mitigating risks like foot and mouth disease managing a large dairy farm only on seeds of gold.
