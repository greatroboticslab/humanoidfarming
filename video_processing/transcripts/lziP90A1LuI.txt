 Have you ever wondered who Demeter is in Greek mythology? Demeter is the goddess of the harvest and agriculture. She is one of the twelve Olympian gods and goddesses. Demeter is the daughter of Cronus and Rhea. Her daughter, Persephone, was kidnapped by Hades. This event caused Demeter great sorrow and affected the Earth's fertility. Because of this, seasons were created to reflect her emotions. Demeter's story explains the changing seasons in Greek mythology. Her symbols include the cornucopia and sheaves of wheat.
