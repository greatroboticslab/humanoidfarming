 If you're a rice lover, you'll want to hear this. Vietnam's small rice farms are going green, and it's changing the game. In Vietnam's Mekong Delta, farmers are embracing sustainable farming practices to protect their land and secure their future. These smallholders are using techniques like alternate wetting and drying to save water without sacrificing their rice yields. By cutting down on pesticides and fertilizers, they're not just preserving the environment, but also protecting their health. These methods are simple yet effective, ensuring long-term food security and a healthier ecosystem. The success of these sustainable practices in the Mekong Delta is inspiring other rice-growing regions around the world to follow suit.
