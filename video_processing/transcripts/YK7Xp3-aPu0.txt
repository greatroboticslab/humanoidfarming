 Agricultural technology is a fast-paced field of continually advancing production agriculture through more precise decision-making and automation. The Agricultural Systems Technology degree program allows students to learn more about this expanding field as well as combine their interest in technology, machines, and business to help them stay at the forefront of technological innovation in agriculture, environmental protection, and related industries. Students study a variety of topics including digital agricultural systems pertaining to structures, hydraulics, precision agriculture, machinery, electricity, sensors, and the Internet of Things. Agricultural Systems Technology students apply science, technology, and engineering principles to manage complex agricultural and other production systems. Faculty within the Agricultural Systems Technology degree program prepare graduates for numerous careers, including agricultural and power equipment manufacturing, equipment sales and service, food production and processing, precision agriculture technology, and government. One of the many unique experiences available for students in the degree program is the chance to earn a certificate in precision agriculture technology. Precision agriculture is a management practice used by farmers to collect data, study variables, develop strategies, and implement plans on their farm.
