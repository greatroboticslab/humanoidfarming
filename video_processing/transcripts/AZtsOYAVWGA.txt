 How we bale 20 to 30 percent moisture hay without burning our barn dump. First I use my 100 gallon Endura-Plast diesel fuel tank and I top off the fuel tank on my tractor. Then I start cutting 13 feet each path, even though it does rain the next day. Sometimes you just gotta take a chance, otherwise you'll never get done. The weathermen were right. Then we take our tether and we blast the hay all over. It spreads it out really nice and it allows the maximum drying. Next I take our rotary rake and rake all the rows single. Try to help it dry better. This step is very important. This is what keeps the hay from spoiling. We take our powder, our dry hay inoculant and mix it with water. Spray it on the hay while we bale. We then wait until about 4 o'clock in the afternoon to get it as dry as possible. And then we go. We have to bale it today because it's staying rain tomorrow. As soon as my dad gets some bales baled, I start moving them and get them off the field.
