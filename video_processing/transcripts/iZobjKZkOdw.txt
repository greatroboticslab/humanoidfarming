Let's see some practical crop rotation techniques in action on the Johnson farm. First, they implemented sequential planting, following corn with legumes to replenish soil nitrogen, enhancing fertility and yields. Next, they adopted strip cropping. By alternating rows of different crops, they controlled pests more effectively and reduced soil erosion. This also increased farm biodiversity, making it more resilient to adverse conditions. Another technique was mixed cropping. By planting different crops together, they maximized land use and improved weed control, encouraging natural pollination and boosting productivity. Finally, they used cover cropping. Planting cover crops like rye or clover during the off-season protected the soil from erosion and maintained its organic content, especially during winter.
