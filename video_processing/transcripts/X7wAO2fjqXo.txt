 Sustainable Agricultural Intensification, SAI, initially aimed to increase agricultural production without harming the environment, but has since evolved to incorporate social, economic, and environmental components of sustainability. While trade-offs between these aspects are likely, synergies are also possible. Agroecological intensification is considered more equitable than technological intensification, but interdisciplinary research is needed to understand potential outcomes and inherent trade-offs of different approaches. This article was authored by Jeremy Hager, Valerie Nelson, Richard Lamble, and others.
