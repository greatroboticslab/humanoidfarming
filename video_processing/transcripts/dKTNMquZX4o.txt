One of the most significant impacts of climate change on coffee production is the increase in temperature. Higher temperatures can lead to reduced yields and lower-quality coffee, as the beans ripen more quickly and lose some of their flavor and aroma. In addition, higher temperatures can make coffee plants more susceptible to pests and diseases, which can further reduce yields and quality. Another impact of climate change on coffee production is the change in rainfall patterns. In some regions, rainfall has become less predictable, leading to longer dry spells and more frequent droughts. This can lead to water stress for coffee plants, which can reduce their productivity and increase their susceptibility to pests and diseases. At the same time, changes in rainfall patterns can also lead to more frequent floods and landslides, which can damage coffee farms and reduce yields.
