 Saturn, one of the most revered gods in Roman mythology, was known as the ruler of time and agriculture. Ancient Romans believed Saturn presided over a golden age of peace and abundance, where people lived in harmony and the land flourished. He was often depicted holding a sickle, symbolizing his connection to the harvest. Saturn's influence was celebrated in the joyful festival of Saturnalia, a time of feasting and gift-giving, reflecting his legacy of prosperity and renewal.
