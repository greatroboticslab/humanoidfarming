 Food Safety for People with Weakened Immune Systems Storing Food Safely In this video you will learn how to store perishable food safely, how to store non-perishable food safely, how to store perishable food safely. Perishable food is food that needs to be stored in a fridge or freezer. Put perishable food in the fridge or freezer as soon as you get home from grocery shopping. Perishable food is more likely to grow harmful germs if not stored properly. Keep perishable food in the fridge or freezer to stop germs from growing in the temperature danger zone. For more information watch the video The Temperature Danger Zone. Examples of perishable foods that need to be stored in a fridge or freezer are milk, eggs, seafood like fish and shellfish, cheese, butter, meat, poultry like chicken and turkey. Before you place food in the freezer label food packages with the date you bought the food. Labeling food helps you keep track of what food you should eat first. Foods can remain safely in a freezer for up to three months. Contact Health Canada or check the Health Canada website for guidelines on storing food in the freezer. Separate raw meat, fish and poultry from other food to limit cross-contamination. Place cooked food and ready to eat foods away from packaged raw meats, poultry like chicken or turkey, or seafood like fish or shellfish. For more information watch the video Cross-Contamination. Once a food package is opened, follow the directions found on the package such as Refrigerate after opening, keep refrigerated. Contact Health Canada or check the Health Canada website for guidelines on how long to keep food in a fridge. Storing non-perishable foods safely Non-perishable foods are foods that don't need to be stored in the fridge or freezer. Here are some ways to safely store non-perishable foods. Seal open dry goods before you store them. Seal open dry goods such as cereal or baking flour before you store them. Use the First In First Out method. The First In First Out method is a way to manage the food in your fridge and cupboard. Move older food items to the front of the fridge or cupboard so that they are used first.
