 Today we will talk about the importance of agriculture. First we will define agriculture. What is agriculture? Agriculture is the production and marketing of food and fiber products, crops and animals. There are two areas of agriculture. The first is production agriculture. It is work done on the farm. Second is agribusiness. It is work done off the farm. Production agriculture also known as farming or ranching. Crops are plants that are grown for its products. Examples of production agriculture are livestock, cattle poultry, sheep and etc. Agribusiness is getting products from the farm to the consumer. Agribusiness also consists of professionals such as vets and chemist. There are also other areas of agriculture. They are horticulture, forestry and natural resources. Horticulture is the production of plants for their beauty and usefulness. Horticulture includes ornamentals and food crops. Products of horticulture are lettuce, apples, nuts and etc. Forestry is the science and art of growing trees and making them into products and tree farms is a farm that specializes in growing trees. Natural resources are water, minerals, wildfire, oil, air, soil. Now we move on to our main topic. Why is agriculture important? Agriculture plays a great role on different aspects of our lives. Agriculture is the backbone of the economic system of a given country. It provides the foundation through which people survive through the provision of food to both humans and their animals. Without agriculture, chances of survival would be minimal as both people and livestock would die out of starvation. Agriculture also reduces poverty by providing both a source of food and employment. In addition to providing food and raw material, agriculture also provides employment opportunities to a very large percentage of the population. Below are the factors why agriculture is important.
