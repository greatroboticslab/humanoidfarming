 As organic farming expands globally, ensuring compliance with international standards is vital for maintaining product quality, environmental sustainability, and consumer confidence. ISO certifications offer a comprehensive framework that helps organic farming companies achieve these goals. Several key ISO standards apply to organic farming. ISO 14001, environmental management, essential for minimizing environmental impacts and promoting sustainable farming practices. ISO 22000, food safety management, crucial for maintaining safety across the food supply chain. ISO 9001, quality management systems, which helps ensure consistent product quality and customer satisfaction. ISO 50001, energy management, enabling companies to improve energy efficiency and reduce carbon footprints. ISO 26000, social responsibility, guiding organizations in ethical and sustainable business practices. ISO 45001, occupational health and safety, ensuring safe working conditions for farm workers.
