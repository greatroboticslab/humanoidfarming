 . Steeped in history and allure, Paradise Valley has been a haven for explorers and nature enthusiasts for generations. Early settlers who ventured into this area were so struck by its lush meadows, towering peaks, and abundant wildlife that they simply named it Paradise. A designation that has endured throughout the centuries. Welcome to the Sitka Ranch. The Sitka Ranch sits at the crossroads of everything desirable in a best-in-class offering. It represents the epitome of natural beauty, with world-class recreational opportunities both on the ranch and nearby. Coupled with a productive and sustainable cattle operation and impeccable improvements, this offering sets the standard for ranches of this stature and caliber. Consisting of approximately 2,971 acres, this Northern Rockies Foothill Ranch is nestled in the southern end of Paradise Valley. The ranch is just 25 minutes from Livingston, an eclectic western town home to writers, artists, and cowboys alike. Livingston provides ample amenities and a public-use airport with full-service FBO. Approximately 30 minutes to the south is Gardner and the entrance to Yellowstone National Park. One of the last and largest nearly intact natural ecosystems in North America. Yellowstone National Park is a natural wonder of epic proportions. Just about 70 minutes away is the vibrant community of Bozeman. Bozeman has received national notoriety for its scenic location and historic downtown area. The Bozeman-Yellowstone International Airport is the busiest in Montana, with non-stop flights to many major destinations. The Sitka Ranch enjoys a diverse and productive landscape. To the north, you'll find gently rolling hills and grasslands dotted with three small ponds and intersected by Mill Creek Ditch as it meanders through the property. To the south, the Sitka Ranch rises up at the base of Green Mountain, with almost 400 acres of dense evergreens and quaking aspens bordering the Absaroki Beartooth Wilderness. With the Gallatin National Forest to the north, the view shed on the ranch is dramatic and striking from every direction. At the heart of the upper ranch, positioned in a private basin with panoramic scenery, is the Owner's Compound, which features a log guest cabin, a historic horse barn, and working cattle facilities in addition to the main home. The Owner's Home spans approximately 13,000 square feet and boasts a tasteful design, featuring large windows that perfectly capture the stunning views. Built in 2006 and remodeled in 2016, this exquisite residence comprises six bedrooms, seven bathrooms, and two half baths. Among its many luxurious amenities are a lap pool and a small theater, adding to the allure of this exceptional property. With a large kitchen, formal dining room, and several common areas, there is ample room to entertain friends and family. The primary working improvements are located on the lower ranch and encompass all the essential elements required for a ranch of this size, such as a large manager's house, horse barn, calving barn, corrals, and shop, to name a few. The structures and infrastructure on the property are meticulously maintained, showcasing a remarkable commitment to detail. Sitka Ranch supports a base herd of approximately 150 animal units as a fully operational cattle ranch. The winter forage is efficiently cultivated on about 463 acres, irrigated with nine pivots and three wheel lines, and utilizing water from Mill Creek Ditch. This proven ranching methodology has been successfully employed throughout the property's history. The recreation opportunities on the ranch and nearby are unparalleled. The three spring-fed ponds have been meticulously rehabilitated to enhance aquatic life for cutthroat and rainbow trout, creating an idyllic setting to cast a line just moments away from the main home. For those craving larger water, the property is just minutes away from access to the Yellowstone River. This renowned fishery lures anglers from around the globe to challenge themselves against its abundant trophy-sized trout. Or explore the numerous world-class spring creeks that Paradise Valley has to offer. As a part of the greater Yellowstone ecosystem, the property serves as a haven for a diverse array of wildlife, with countless species calling it home or passing through its expansive boundaries. Waterfowl and upland birds flourish in this area, while herds of elk, mule deer, and white-tailed deer find solace in the cover, water, and abundant feed provided by this rich environment. The bordering and nearby national forests provide a lifetime of exploration and additional hunting opportunities. Sitka Ranch offers a truly unique opportunity to coexist with nature on a grand scale. Here, productive ranching, recreation, and natural beauty converge to create an extraordinary sanctuary that guarantees a lasting legacy of cherished memories.
