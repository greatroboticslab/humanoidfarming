To plow a garden, make your first furrow right down the middle from end to end. Then turn around and begin your next pass by putting the right rear tire of your tractor in the furrow you just made. You'll continue plowing this way, always with the right rear tire in the furrow and always turning right in an enlarging pattern until your garden is plowed. In this way, the plow always throws soil into the furrow to the right of your tractor. Because your right rear tire is always in the furrow, you may need to adjust a third arm and lift length to make sure your plow stays level from front to back and side to side, which is key to turning the soil upside down and rolling it into the furrow to the right. Once you plowed the ground, it's a good idea to let that soil dry out and settle down before you do anything else. In fact, it's not a bad idea at all to plow your garden in the fall and just let that soil sit over the winter, letting any plant material break down and fertilize the soil.
