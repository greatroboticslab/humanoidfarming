Pets & Animals
 The Damascus goat, also known as Aleppo or Shami, boasts a fascinating history. Originating in Syria, this breed was introduced to Cyprus by the Antoniades family and British settlers in the 1920s. Selective breeding in Cyprus has enhanced its qualities, making it a prized source of both milk and meat. The breed's significance caught the attention of the Food and Agriculture Organization, which prioritizes its preservation. Over the years, the Damascus goat has become synonymous with agricultural heritage in the Levant region. Its remarkable adaptability and productivity have solidified its status as a vital livestock breed. This rich history not only showcases the goat's agricultural importance, but also its cultural significance in the regions it inhabits.
