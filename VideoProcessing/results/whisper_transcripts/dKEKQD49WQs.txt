 Are you looking to increase your sweet corn production? Here's three things you can do right now to help boost your yields. Make sure your corn gets plenty of water. Having your corn on a dedicated irrigation system makes sure that the plant has enough water when it starts to tassel and put on ears. In addition to water, corn needs plenty of nitrogen to grow. I like to side dress mine with a high nitrogen fertilizer once when it's about knee high and once when it starts to tassel. I like to side dress my corn with ammonium sulfate. It's a 21-0-0 fertilizer, meaning it's 21% nitrogen, 0% phosphorus, and 0% potassium. The last thing you can do is hill up dirt around the base of the plants. This makes the corn plant sturdier and also allows it to take up more water and nutrients, creating a stronger, healthier corn stock. equilibrium water iscalmed Central and the entire plant.
