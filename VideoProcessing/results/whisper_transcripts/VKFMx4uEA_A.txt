 Planting dates for vegetables in Louisiana Most of Louisiana falls into the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Plant Hardiness Zone 8. Gardeners have a long growing season to enjoy, and planting at the correct times can help take complete advantage of it. Vegetables such as asparagus, turnips, cabbage, carrots, garden peas, Irish potatoes, spinach and radishes can be planted as early as January 15. A month later, beets and broccoli can follow, and beginning at the end of March gardeners can plant watermelon, tomato, corn and cantaloupe. On April 1st, vegetables like peppers, okra, cucumbers, eggplant, limo beans, whole beans and squash can be planted. Sweet potatoes should be planted last, around mid-April. Considerations Considerations Considerations Small areas on the Gulf Coast fall into Zone 9, including cities like New Orleans and Lake Charles. In this area, vegetables are divided into warm season and cool season. Warm season vegetables like sweet corn, okra, southern peas and watermelon can be planted in March, while cool season vegetables like carrots, cabbage and leeks can be planted in September.
