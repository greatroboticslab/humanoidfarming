The crop is typically ready for harvest seven to nine months after planting, when the leaves and stems of the plant start to turn yellow and dry out. In India, turmeric harvesting generally occurs between December and March, depending on the region. The harvesting process begins with the cutting of the plant tops, leaving only the rhizomes in the soil. These rhizomes are then carefully dug out using spades or mechanical harvesters. The harvested rhizomes are gathered and cleaned to remove soil and other impurities. After harvesting, turmeric undergoes a meticulous preservation process. First, the locals wash the turmeric with water to remove any remaining mud and dirt. Next, the turmeric is neatly arranged in pots and placed on the stove to cook. This cooking process takes place directly over the fire until the turmeric is fully cooked. Once done, the turmeric is spread out on the ground to dry under the sun. When it's completely dry, the turmeric is put into a rotating machine. The machine removes the outer layer, revealing the bright golden color inside, which looks very appealing. Finally, the turmeric is carefully packaged, allowing it to be preserved for several years without losing its quality.
