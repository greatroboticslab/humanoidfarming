 Here is how you are going to start a farm if you have no money in 2025. My qualifications are I am doing this exactly without a tractor on 6.74 acres of land that I bought with a loan. First thing you're going to do if you want this to be you is understand that you need less than an acre to feed your family and have surplus for a farm stand or a farmer's market. You do not need to buy 10, 20, or 30 acres. I barely bought seven and all I am using is one of them, even less than one. Find the land you want to buy, have it be 15, 20, 30 minutes outside of a city center you want to be near. Your first step is you're either going to get a loan for the land, which is going to allow you to free up capital or at least purchase the land if you don't have a lot of money. Or if you can't qualify for a loan, there are 0% down programs with the USDA, but you have another option. Seller financing with the farmers themselves. Many of them are over the age of 60 and they don't want to manage 100 acres anymore either. I cannot tell you how many people have written to me telling me they are renting five acres from a farmer for $500 a month. How do you find these farmers? They are in town. You could run a newspaper ad, post in a Facebook group, visit their farm stands, chat with them. $24 trillion worth of farmland is going to transfer hands over the next 20 years, which means yes, these deals are actually available. Once you own the land, you do not need to buy a $100,000 tractor. I'm doing all of this with my two hands and a wheelbarrow. And you know what? It's kind of nice, honestly. It's like therapy in the gym all rolled into one. As for seeds, there are a lot of local community farms that give these seeds away for free. I also give seeds away for free at my farm stand.
