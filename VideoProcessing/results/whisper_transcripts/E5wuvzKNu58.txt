 Did you know missionary societies were among the first to introduce sustainable farming techniques worldwide? In the 19th century, these organizations didn't just spread religious teachings, they also brought agricultural education to remote regions. Missionaries taught crop rotation, soil management, and new tools, helping local communities improve yields and preserve their land. Their efforts laid the foundation for modern agricultural development, empowering farmers to adapt to changing environments, and ensuring food security for generations to come.
