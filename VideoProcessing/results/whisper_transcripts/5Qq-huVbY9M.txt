 From chaos to civilization. How it all began. In early history, tribes roamed freely. They depended on nature's resources. Then, 10,000 years ago, the agricultural revolution sparked change. It changed survival to stability. Farming enabled communities to settle. Cities like Uruk and Mohenjo-Daro emerged, full of activity. Agriculture brought new challenges. Cooperation necessitated self-governing laws. Writing was invented to record stories. Knowledge spread, shaping cultures. Trade, creativity, and ideas flourish. They built towering structures and monumental art. Each step brought them closer to the wonders of civilization. Rituals and governance took form, showcasing humanity's unity. The legacy of this era is profound. We inherited a tapestry woven with dreams and aspirations. Today, echoes of those first farmers remind us of our roots.
