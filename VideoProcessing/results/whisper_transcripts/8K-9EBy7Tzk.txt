Adam Smith's definition of economics often referred to as the wealth definition, views economics primarily as the study of how nations can create wealth. Smith's definition is rooted in his seminal work, The Wealth of Nations, where he defined economics as the science of wealth production, science of wealth production and the mechanisms that contribute to it. According to Smith, the wealth of a nation is created through the accumulation of goods and services and economics should focus on how to maximize wealth of a country. However, this definition has been subject to criticism over time and several drawbacks have been identified. Narrow focus on material wealth. Smith's definition limits economics to the production accumulation of material wealth, production and accumulation of material wealth often overlooking non-material aspects of human welfare. In his time, the goal of economic activity was largely associated with increasing physical goods and services.
