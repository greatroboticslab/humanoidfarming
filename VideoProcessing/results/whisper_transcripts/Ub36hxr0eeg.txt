Help your students determine the ideal mechanical advantage of pulleys by counting the number of supporting ropes. More supporting ropes means a greater mechanical advantage and less force is required to lift the load. A single fixed pulley has just one supporting rope, since the side of the rope that you're pulling on doesn't directly support the load that's being lifted. However, with a single movable pulley, both sides of the rope are supporting the load that's being lifted. So the mechanical advantage is two, and only one newton of force is required to lift a two newton weight. Those examples are usually pretty easy for students to understand, but when fixed and movable pulleys are combined, it can sometimes get a little confusing.
