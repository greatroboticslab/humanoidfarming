 Forests are much more than just trees. Covering a third of the Earth's land, they play a vital role in preserving the water cycle, conserving soil and protecting biodiversity. By storing carbon, they are a front-line defense against the effects of climate change. For many, they also provide food, fuel, shelter and income. Yet forest cover has been shrinking at an alarming rate, with a net loss of 3.3 million hectares per year. Much of it, about 80%, is cleared to make room for agriculture. As the world's population continues to increase, demand for food will also rise. The challenge will be to feed 9 billion people while also preventing further loss of forest land. The experiences of more than 20 countries show that increasing forest cover and improving food security can go hand in hand, and that forests, in turn, can help communities to prosper. Forest cover has now expanded to 54% thanks to more sustainable agriculture practices, stronger legal controls over land use, and especially funding for forest protection.
