 So vacation year is 16 months of not growing any cash crop. The goal of the vacation year is about growing fertility, about growing soil. So our rotation right now is that every field is on vacation every other year. So at any one moment in time, half the farm will be in vegetable production and the other half will be in vacation. The vacation starts in the fall of the cash crop year and then we plant a winter cover crop, a combination of a grain crop, rye, wheat or barley, sometimes oats with a legume. Crimson clover is our favorite. Sometimes we use vetch. Then we'll let that grow quite a long time. Then that gets flail mowed and then spaded in. And then a summer cover crop comes on. My favorite are cowpeas and Sudan grass. And of course, buckwheat is always easy and it comes up well. And then we plant a winter cover crop.
