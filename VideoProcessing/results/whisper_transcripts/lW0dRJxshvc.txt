 Monoculture is an agriculture technique in which one crop is grown in a given area. Some problems with monoculture is that it drains nutrients from the soil, lowers plant diversity, and forces small town farmers off their land. A potential solution to monoculture is crop rotation. Crop rotation is where you plant different crops in a specific sequence. Some benefits of crop rotation include the improvement of soil nutrient levels, such as increased nitrogen levels, and also helps reduce the amount of weeds and insects in an area. A successful use of crop rotation can lead to sustainable agriculture. Sustainable agriculture can be defined as long-term profit over time, a higher quality of life for farmers, and an increased stewardship for the land.
