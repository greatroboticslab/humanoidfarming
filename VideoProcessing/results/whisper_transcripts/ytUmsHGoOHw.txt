Why Life Insurance Matters After 60 Many people think life insurance is only for the young, but that's a myth. Here's why life insurance after 60 can still be incredibly valuable. On-screen bullets. Cover funeral and final expenses. Leave a legacy for children or grandchildren. Pay off debts or mortgage. Help a spouse maintain lifestyle. Fund charitable giving. And most importantly, it offers peace of mind. Best types of life insurance for seniors over 60. Now let's explore the best types of policies available after 60. 1. Final expense insurance. Burial insurance. Covers $5,000 to $30,000. No medical exam. Affordable monthly premium. Fast approval. 2. Guaranteed universal life. GUL. Fixed premiums. Coverage till age 90, 95, or even 100. More affordable than whole life. 3. Term life insurance. Great for ages 60 to 65. Lower cost. Expires after 10, 15, or 20 years. Good for covering temporary needs. 4. Whole life insurance. Limited use. Lifetime coverage. Builds cash value. Higher premiums. What affects life insurance costs after 60? Premiums go up as we age. But here's what really impacts the cost. Age. 60 to 69 vs 70 to 79. Health conditions. E.g. diabetes. Heart issues. Tobacco use. Coverage amount. Type of policy. For example, a 60-year-old in good health may pay $80 per month for $25,000 in coverage. A 70-year-old smoker might pay over $200 per month. How to choose the right policy after 60? Narrator. When selecting a policy, ask yourself. What is the purpose? Funeral expenses, debt, or inheritance? Do I want term or lifelong coverage? What monthly premium fits my budget? Do I qualify for no exam policies? A licensed agent can help you compare rates from multiple providers based on your unique situation. Best life insurance companies for seniors, 2025.
