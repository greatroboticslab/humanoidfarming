Use your nose and thermometer. Test moisture content. Dry matter intake is key. Wet haylage dilutes nutrients, so balance rations accordingly. Introduce gradually. Switching from hay to haylage? Watch for dust and mold. Dusty or moldy hay can reduce intake and cause respiratory issues. Visually inspect each bale. Nutritional and production impacts. Haylage. Due to fermentation, haylage often retains more nutrients, particularly proteins and sugars. Cows may find it more palatable, which can lead to higher dry matter intake and increased milk yields. Hay. More stable in storage and safer if managed well. It's typically used during dry seasons or where silage making is impractical. Haylage-fed cows can sometimes show improved digestibility, energy intake, and butterfat levels. Cost, storage, and labor considerations. Haylage requires more investment, machinery, wrapping, and careful feed-out management, but can lead to better feed efficiency. Hay is simpler and cheaper to store, but more weather-dependent and potentially lower in digestible nutrients.
